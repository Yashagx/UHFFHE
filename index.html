About Us

Hello from a Student's Heart

Hi! I’m a student at the United Forum for Health & Environment Institute — a place that offers hope and learning every evening to children like me.

Many of us come from humble backgrounds, but here we are respected, encouraged, and taught skills that open new dreams.

Guided by Meena Mukherjee Ma’am (Head), Gudiya Nag Ma’am, and Priya Nag Ma’am, this institute feels like a second home where I belong.

We learn computers, creativity, and caring for our world—and we even made this website ourselves!

Our Mentors

Meena Mukherjee

Head & Guide

With years of experience in education and leadership, Meena Ma'am plays a pivotal role as the head and guide of our learning community. Her vision, dedication, and mentorship inspire both students and teachers alike to strive for excellence. She ensures that each child receives the right guidance and nurtures their curiosity and creativity.

Gudiya Nag

Senior Class Teacher

Gudiya Ma'am specializes in teaching senior class students. She has a unique ability to connect with older students, helping them build strong foundations in academics as well as life skills. Her engaging teaching methods and subject expertise make complex topics easier to understand, while motivating students to push their limits.

Priya Nag

Junior Class Teacher

Priya Ma'am focuses on nurturing and guiding the younger students in the junior classes. Her warm and friendly approach makes learning fun and interactive for small children. With her patience and creative teaching techniques, she helps students take their first steps towards education with confidence and joy.

Yash Agarwal Sir

Yash Agarwal

SRM student • My Programming Mentor

Yash Sir, a friendly and passionate mentor from SRM Institute of Science and Technology, made our learning experience really special. He taught us from the end of May till the first week of July, and in this short time, he created many happy learning moments for us. His teaching style was fun and interactive, and every session felt like an exciting learning journey. With interesting quizzes, fun games, and hands-on programming tasks, he turned our evenings into something we always looked forward to. He often surprised us with gifts and chocolates whenever we performed well, which made us even more excited to participate and give our best. His sessions made us think more, ask questions, and enjoy learning about coding..

Chart Work

Our young students from Classes 1 to 5 used their creativity and imagination to create beautiful charts on important social topics. Each chart reflects their innocent thoughts and strong messages towards building a better and equal society.

Beti Ko Padhana Hai

This inspiring chart highlights the importance of educating girls. Through drawings and slogans, our students shared the message that every girl deserves the right to education and a brighter future.

Gender Equality

With colorful artwork and simple words, the children spoke about treating boys and girls equally. Their chart promotes fairness, respect, and equal opportunities for everyone.

Girl Child Equality Rights

This thoughtful poster focuses on giving equal rights to every girl child—whether it’s education, health, or opportunities in life. The messages drawn by our students reflect hope, equality, and care for all girls.

Activities

International Yoga Day Celebration

On the eve of International Yoga Day (21st June), students of United Forum for Health & Environment participated enthusiastically in a Yoga Training Camp conducted in association with Patanjali trainers. From Suryanamaskar to Sarwangasan, students practiced major yoga poses with energy and dedication. Our General Secretary, Meena Mukherjee, thanked the trainers and appreciated the children's spirit and participation.
